I started working on computers with an old Texas Instrument, which I used to create simple loop programs. Then a few years after that we got my Uncle's Tandy 1000, which was a great upgrade and had a color monitor. I started learning QBasic, and playing games. My programs also became more advanced as I learned conditional statements, and more advanced looping techniques.
In tenth grade, the high school upgraded their computer system. Since my Dad was a teacher there, I was able to ride home with him and stay after school. Staying after let me learn about fixing issues there.
Some where is an old newspaper clipping of me in the new computer lab. I also helped the teachers with things when the Network Administrator was not there, which earned me the title "The Wizard".

My lack of employment has allowed me to develop another interest in woodworking. I gather scraps of wood from local trailer factories, that would otherwise be thrown out. I use the larger scraps of wood to build desks, shelves, and I even built a table and four chair set for my daughter.
